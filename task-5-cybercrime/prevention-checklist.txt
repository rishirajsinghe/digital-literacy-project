Stay Safe Online - Prevention Checklist

1. Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or bank details with anyone, no matter how genuine they sound. Banks or officials will never ask for this information.
2. Don’t accept unknown “collect requests” on UPI apps. Entering your PIN means you are sending money, not receiving it.
3. Always double-check payment requests before approving them. If something feels suspicious, cancel it and confirm with the sender separately.
4. Avoid clicking on suspicious links from emails, messages, or social media, especially if they ask for personal or financial details.
5. Use strong, unique passwords for all your accounts and enable two-factor authentication (2FA) for extra security.
6. Do not install apps from unknown sources or allow screen-sharing access to strangers.
7. Avoid using public Wi-Fi for banking or financial transactions. Use mobile data or a secure network instead.
8. Keep your phone and apps updated regularly to fix security vulnerabilities.
9. Be cautious of messages or calls that create urgency (like “account blocked” or “limited-time offer”). Scammers often use panic to trick users.
10. If you face cyber fraud, report it immediately on cybercrime.gov.in or call the helpline 1930.
